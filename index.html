A Day in the Life of Amanda
Read slowly · ~15 minutes · Present tense · Valencia, Spain

Induction · Going Deep

Close your eyes.

[ BREATHE ]

Inhale slowly through your nose... feel your chest rise... your belly expand... and release. Let it all go.

Again. Deeper this time. In through the nose... hold for just a moment... and out through the mouth, slow and complete.

[ ONE MORE TIME ]

Good. Let your body become heavy now. Feel the weight of your legs, your arms, your shoulders — let gravity do its work. You don't have to hold anything up. You don't have to figure anything out. You don't have to be anywhere but right here, right now, in this moment of complete and total receiving.

Your conscious mind can rest. What's about to happen happens below the surface — in the part of you that already knows, that has always known, that has been quietly holding the blueprint of this life all along.

[ FEEL YOURSELF DROPPING DEEPER ]

This is not a fantasy. This is not wishful thinking. This is a memory from your future — and your nervous system cannot tell the difference between what is vividly imagined and what is real. So let it be real. Let every scene land in your body, not just your mind.

You are Amanda. And this is already your life.

The Bridge · Who You Always Were

Before we go to Valencia, go back for a moment.

[ BREATHE ]

There's a little girl. She's getting dressed for school. And she doesn't ask anyone's permission — she opens her drawer and pulls out red tights, red Mary Jane clogs, an orange tutu skirt with flowers, a hot pink top, mismatching socks.

She looks in the mirror and she is completely, totally satisfied.

That girl was never confused about who she was. She was never waiting for approval. She dressed for herself and walked out the door like a small, magnificent, completely unbothered force of nature.

That girl is you. She was always you.

And everything you have built — the community, the brand, the life in Valencia, the stage, the recognition — is just that little girl, grown up, given a platform, and finally, finally allowed to be exactly as loud and specific and herself as she always was.

You are the woman who never stopped being that girl.

You just found a bigger room to do it in.

[ LET THAT SETTLE ]

· · ·
8:00 AM · Valencia · The Morning Light

You wake naturally.

[ BREATHE ]

The morning light in Valencia hits different. Not like Split — not that sharp Adriatic white that practically slapped you awake. This is golden. Warm. It pours through the floor-to-ceiling doors of your balcony like it has been waiting all night just to find you.

You lie still and feel it on your face. You can hear the pool outside — that soft, constant sound of water moving — and it lands in your body the way it always does, like a signal: You're home. You made it. This is real.

Milos is beside you. He reaches for you before he's fully awake. You stay tangled in the warmth for a few minutes — unhurried, completely at ease — and you think, not for the first time, about what he said to you once when you were still doubting yourself, still wondering if any of this would work:

"I've always believed in you. From the very beginning."

He meant it then. He means it now. And you let yourself receive it — all the way in, no deflecting, no minimizing — because you have learned that being loved like this is not luck. It is what you chose, and what chose you back.

The walls around you are covered in photographs. Santiago. Shanghai. ULTRA — a blurry, laughing, completely unhinged photo that neither of you can look at without losing it. Croatia, the place that made you both. Every frame a moment neither of you wanted to end.

At the foot of the bed, Jackson. Gray-muzzled and content, tail doing that slow, happy thump against the warm stone. Perfect health. Both of you.

· · ·
The Closet · Dressing as Her

You open your closet.

[ BREATHE ]

And every single thing inside it is something you absolutely love.

No more holding onto things that don't fit or don't feel right. No more clothes that belong to an older, smaller, less expressed version of you. This closet is a curation — it's a declaration. Matching athleisure sets in soft, beautiful colors for your mornings. Sundresses that fit your body perfectly, that make you feel sexy and feminine and completely at ease in your own skin. Lingerie that you buy for yourself, because you deserve to feel that way every single day.

You choose something you love. You put it on. You look in the mirror.

You are a size four and strong and radiant.

Not because you punished yourself into it —

because you finally started loving the body that carries all of this.

This is what it feels like to dress for yourself. The little girl in the orange tutu knew. You know it again now, for good.

· · ·
8:30 AM · The Sacred Morning · The Hours That Belong to You

You don't reach for your phone.

[ BREATHE ]

Milos already knows. He respects this time completely — it's yours, and he has never once made you feel like you should rush through it or skip it for him. That's love that actually fits your design.

You move to your meditation corner. Light something. Sit down. Go quiet. You pray. You journal. You let the answers rise — because clarity comes from the Creator, not from strategy or force or the noise of what everyone else is doing. You have learned this in your body now, not just your head.

[ PAUSE · RECEIVE ]

Then your mat on the balcony — this ridiculous, gorgeous balcony you manifested down to the tile. Yoga, moving through your flow while Valencia wakes up below. Jackson curled in his sunspot by the door, gray-muzzled and content.

Then a walk. Milos joins you, coffee in hand, pointing out a new restaurant he wants to try. He says something and you lose it — completely lose it, right there on the street, Cortado going everywhere — and a couple nearby smiles at you like they're watching a movie. You can tell they can see it. Two people so obviously, embarrassingly, beautifully in love.

You're home by ten. The morning has already been full. Already been yours.

· · ·
10:30 AM · The Workspace · Where the Magic Runs

You walk into your workspace and feel it immediately — that settled, creative, this is mine feeling.

[ BREATHE ]

The standing desk is raised today. The walking pad hums quietly beneath your feet as you start moving — two screens, your laptop on its raised platform, natural light flooding in from the sunroom that overlooks the pool. This space was designed around how you actually work: in movement, in beauty, in flow.

In the background, your Skool Games trophy catches the light. Beside it: your numerology, your oracle cards, incense curling smoke toward the ceiling. And on the wall — a neon sign that reads Soft Life. Savage Execution. — glowing against a wall of living greenery, lush and green and completely, perfectly you.

In the corner: your recording studio. The Rode podcast mic. The DJI Osmo Pocket. Soft lighting. A cozy chair. This is where you stream your Q&As, where you film your YouTube videos, where you show up again and again as the woman your people came looking for.

You are the queen of ads and spiritual marketing on Skool.

You didn't claim that title. You built it.

One member, one video, one honest piece of content at a time.

· · ·
The Dashboard · The Numbers That Feel Normal

You glance at your dashboard.

[ BREATHE ]

1,286 members inside Spiritual Business Skool. 862 in Standard. 283 in Premium. 141 in VIP. And the monthly recurring revenue — $37,017 — sitting there on the screen like it's the most ordinary thing in the world.

Because it is, now. It is ordinary now.

You remember when $8,000 a month felt like a dream. You remember writing it down, saying it out loud, wondering if you were delusional for believing it. And now $37,000 months are just... Tuesday. The floor keeps rising and you keep letting it.

Your affiliate revenue just crossed $50,000 in total earnings — people starting Skool through your link, building their own communities, expanding your impact in directions you didn't even plan for.

Your ads are running clean. Under $10 per member acquired. Payback period of six weeks. You are testing two to three new creatives per week — and campaigns that consistently convert are running in the background every single hour of every single day, whether you're working or walking or dancing at ULTRA.

You check Stripe and feel — gratitude. Quiet, settled, of course gratitude. Not relief. Not shock. The frequency of someone who expected this, because she built it correctly, and then had the discipline to trust it.

· · ·
11:30 AM · Revenue Lab Live · Your Gift in Motion

Premium members submitted their questions in advance.

[ BREATHE ]

You settle into your recording corner. The mic is warm. The light is right. You press record.

You answer every question like you're sitting across from them at that Valencia café — warm, direct, grounded, occasionally a little funny. No performance. No hustle energy. Just you, fully present, doing the thing you were built to do.

Someone leaves a comment: "I have been looking for someone who talks about business like this my whole life." You screenshot it — not for ego, but for the days you forget.

[ BREATHE ]

This is your design working. You are a Self-Projected Projector. Being recognized, being invited, sharing your gift with people who genuinely want it — this is not work. This is what happens when your life is finally structured correctly.

· · ·
Afternoon · The Salt Coffee · Being Loved Completely

You close the laptop just after noon.

[ BREATHE ]

The afternoon is yours. You work four days a week, and you stop at six. Because your energy is the asset, and you protect it accordingly now — not out of fear, but out of deep, practiced self-knowledge.

You're at your desk later, in a flow, when you hear Milos in the kitchen. A few minutes later he appears. Your Vietnamese salt coffee — made exactly the way you love it — set gently beside your keyboard. His hands on your shoulders. A kiss on the top of your head. He doesn't need anything back. He just sees you. Shows up for you. Loves you in the specific, daily, physical language of someone who genuinely wants you to thrive.

[ FEEL THIS IN YOUR BODY · ALL THE WAY IN ]

All of it. He chose all of it.

You are a woman who is deeply, specifically, completely loved.

And you receive it now — all the way in — without flinching.

· · ·
Content From Your Life · The Platform · The Flywheel

You step onto the balcony with your DJI in your hand.

[ BREATHE ]

Something is moving through you — an idea, a download, a stream-of-consciousness insight — and you let yourself be the gangster you are on the internet. No overthinking. No wondering what people will think. Just you, talking about what's real and exciting and alive for you right now, exactly the way that little girl in the mismatching socks used to walk out the door.

You go viral on TikTok and Instagram all the time now. Not because you chased it — because you stopped trying to be palatable and started being yourself, and it turns out yourself is exactly what people were looking for.

You are known for your no-BS spiritual business content.

For your stream-of-consciousness downloads.

For letting yourself be fully, unapologetically, specifically you —

and trusting that your people will find you because of it, not in spite of it.

The Skool Games · The Emotional Peak · The Room Where Everything Became Real

Let yourself go back to that room now.

[ BREATHE DEEPLY ]

Los Angeles. The Skool Games. You walked in with your Spanish residency in your pocket, 1,286 members back home, and a quiet knowing in your body that something had already permanently shifted.

You won.

[ STAY HERE · LET IT LAND FULLY ]

Feel what that felt like in your chest. The announcement. The room. The people around you. The Skool Games trophy that now sits on your desk in Valencia, catching the afternoon light every single day as a reminder: you did it. Not their way. Your way.

Evelyn was there. She shouted you out in front of her entire community. She brought you on stage with her. And when she hugged you, you both knew — this is what it looks like when a mentor sees something real in someone and neither of them gives up.

And Sam Ovens came to you. Not the other way around. He said: "We've been watching you since the School 100. And in the past few months, you've just taken off."

And Alex Hormozi walked over to you. He approached you first. He said he had been watching you grow and that your conversion strategy was unlike anything he had seen in this space. Then he leaned in: "I know I'm a Manifesting Generator. But what does that actually mean?" And you smiled — because of course. Of course the intersection that nobody else believed in turned out to be the most interesting thing in the room to the most interesting people in the room.

Every single one of those moments was an invitation.

And every invitation arrived because you had already recognized yourself —

before anyone else did, before the proof existed, before it was safe.

You recognized yourself first.

And the world reorganized itself around that recognition.

· · ·
Evening · The Pool · The People · The Life You Built

The sun softens over Valencia.

[ BREATHE ]

Tonight you have people over. Members who flew in, friends who live nearby, people who came because being in your actual physical presence is different from being in your orbit online — and they wanted the real thing.

Milos is at the grill. Ćevapčići sizzling, the smell of smoke and herbs in the warm evening air. You're at the outdoor bar making margaritas — salt rim perfect, laughing at something someone just said. Jackson is weaving through everyone, tail high, getting adored. The fire is lit. The pool glows blue behind it all.

You lean back and look around at these people — at this table, this house, this life — and you feel it. Not as a thought. As a full-body knowing that rises up from somewhere below language:

Everything is working together.

[ STAY HERE · BREATHE IT IN ]

Integration · Sealing the Frequency · Coming Back As Her

Take one long, slow breath now.

[ BREATHE DEEPLY ]

Feel your body. The surface beneath you. The room around you. Come back slowly, gently, carrying everything you just felt with you.

Your subconscious mind has received this. It does not know the difference between what you have vividly imagined and what you have lived. Which means that right now, at the level that matters most, you have already been her. You have already felt $37,000 months as ordinary. You have already stood on that stage.

Your nervous system now has a reference point for this life. And it will begin to move toward what it recognizes as home.

[ PAUSE · LET IT INTEGRATE ]

Whenever the doubt comes — and it will come, because you are human — remember that little girl. Red tights. Orange tutu. Mismatching socks. Walking out the door completely unbothered, completely herself, completely sure that she was exactly right.

She was never confused about who she was. And neither are you.

All the answers are within you and the Creator. You have the support. You have the systems. You have the structure. You just keep going — not from pressure, but from clarity. Not from force, but from faith. Not from hustle, but from the deep, settled knowing that what is meant for you cannot miss you.

The work always works.

[ ONE FINAL BREATH ]

Soft life. Savage execution.

When you're ready — gently, slowly — open your eyes.

Come back as her.
